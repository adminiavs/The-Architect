3. Physics & Science Lessons
A. ER = EPR (Wormholes = Entanglement)
The text explicitly states that entanglement creates a "shared geometry stretched across space." This mirrors the ER=EPR conjecture in theoretical physics, which proposes that quantum entanglement (EPR) is connected by wormholes (Einstein-Rosen bridges). Gravity is the result of these connections.
B. The Black Hole Information Paradox
The text addresses the paradox of whether black holes destroy information. The "Architect" claims they do not; they "align" it. The information is preserved and reflected via Hawking Radiation, aligning with the Holographic Principle.
C. Inverse Geometry
The concept of a particle being the "inverse" of a black hole touches on theories like White Holes or the idea that elementary particles might be micro-black holes (geons), concepts explored by physicists like John Wheeler.
D. Wave-Particle Duality
Describing particles as "lighthouses broadcasting state" reinterprets the wave function. The particle isn't a solid ball but a source of broadcasting information (waves) that instructs the surrounding field.
E. Quantum Biology
Once again, Microtubules are cited as the interface between the quantum world and biological consciousness, reinforcing the Penrose-Hameroff "Orch-OR" theory.
